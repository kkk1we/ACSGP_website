Sustainable energy

Hydrogen fuel is a potential renewable energy alternative, as when consumed, it only releases water vapour. Liquid hydrogen and oxygen is currently used in rocket propulsion. However, we are working on hydrogen fuel cells a different way to harness hydrogen fuel, which does not involve combustion, and directly generates electricity through proton exchange membranes. We are currently working on altering various operating perimeters to enhance the cell's efficiency. 

Mechanical engineering

Our project involves making a 1:10 car travel approximately 70km over a 6 hour period. This feat requires meticulous attention to detail in the design, construction, and testing of various vehicular mechanisms. Key focus areas are aerodynamics optimisation, weight reduction and material efficiency, precision manufacturing using various systems and energy efficiency. For this project, this will involve choosing parts carefully with an understanding of where energy is expanded. For optimal weight efficiency, we will also be manufacturing custom parts. 

Software engineering

For this project to succeed, a lot of testing and monitoring of race conditions is required. For testing, we have built a "treadmill" to run the car in race conditions, and we plan to install  many sensors and program microcontrollers to carefully monitor and optimise the car's performance. During the race, to ensure we can maximise our distance, we have plans for an energy management system, and a remote telemetry system. These two systems will show us vital data such as power output(w), fuel cell efficiency, temperature, and remaining hydrogen fuel. The race will require us to make about 20 pit-stops, and these systems will help us optimise the pit-stops.

Giving back

From November 9 to 15, we travelled to Baray, Cambodia, on a service learning trip, to see what we could do for the local community there. There, we conducted home visits and delivered care packages to over 50 families, put up solar lamps, built pavements and raised money for orphans.

During this trip, we visited Wisdom nest school, a school committed to giving children quality education for less than USD30 a month. We share their vision of giving quality education to the children of Baray, and at this juncture, we are raising funds to help them procure equipment to conduct practical lessons for secondary level students.
